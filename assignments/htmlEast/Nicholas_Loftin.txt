					<div id="mw-content-text" class="mw-body-content mw-content-ltr" lang="en" dir="ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div class="shortdescription nomobile noexcerpt noprint searchaux" style="display:none">American hip hop record producer</div>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1033289096">.mw-parser-output .hatnote{font-style:italic}.mw-parser-output div.hatnote{padding-left:1.6em;margin-bottom:0.5em}.mw-parser-output .hatnote i{font-style:normal}.mw-parser-output .hatnote+link+.hatnote{margin-top:-0.5em}</style><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about American hip hop record producer. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Nick_Fury_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Nick Fury (disambiguation)">Nick Fury (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1126788409">.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul{line-height:inherit;list-style:none;margin:0;padding:0}.mw-parser-output .plainlist ol li,.mw-parser-output .plainlist ul li{margin-bottom:0}</style><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1066479718">.mw-parser-output .infobox-subbox{padding:0;border:none;margin:-3px;width:auto;min-width:100%;font-size:100%;clear:none;float:none;background-color:transparent}.mw-parser-output .infobox-3cols-child{margin:auto}.mw-parser-output .infobox .navbar{font-size:100%}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-header,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-subheader,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-above,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-title,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-image,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-full-data,body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output .infobox-below{text-align:center}</style><table class="infobox vcard plainlist"><tbody><tr><th colspan="2" class="infobox-above" style="background-color: #b0c4de; font-size: 125%;"><div class="fn">Nick "Fury" Loftin</div></th></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Birth name</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Nicholaus Gerard Loftin</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label"><span class="nowrap">Also known as</span></th><td class="infobox-data nickname">Nick Fury</td></tr><tr><th scope="row" class="infobox-label">Born</th><td class="infobox-data"><a href="/wiki/Newark,_New_Jersey" title="Newark, New Jersey">Newark, New Jersey</a>, U.S.; <p><b>Nicholas Gerard Loftin</b>, professionally known by his <a href="/wiki/Nickname" title="Nickname">moniker</a> as <b>Nick Fury</b> (or simply <b>Fury</b>), is an American hip hop <a href="/wiki/Record_producer" title="Record producer">record producer</a>. He is known for producing <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">Houston</a>-based rapper <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Flip" title="Lil&#39; Flip">Lil' Flip</a>'s 2004 hit single "<a href="/wiki/Game_Over_(Flip)" title="Game Over (Flip)">Game Over (Flip)</a>", which peaked at number 15 in the US <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a> and was certified Platinum by the <a href="/wiki/Recording_Industry_Association_of_America" title="Recording Industry Association of America">Recording Industry Association of America</a> for selling 1,000,000 copies in the United States alone.
</p><p>In January 1996, Loftin relocated to <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">Atlanta</a>, <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">Georgia</a> to produce <a href="/wiki/Soul_music" title="Soul music">soul</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> and <a href="/wiki/Contemporary_R%26B" title="Contemporary R&amp;B">R&amp;B</a> music. He has produced for a variety of high-profile artists, including <a href="/wiki/Lil%27_Kim" title="Lil&#39; Kim">Lil' Kim</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wu-Tang_Clan" title="Wu-Tang Clan">Wu-Tang Clan</a>, <a href="/wiki/T.I." title="T.I.">T.I.</a>, <a href="/wiki/E-40" title="E-40">E-40</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Cannon" title="Nick Cannon">Nick Cannon</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trina" title="Trina">Trina</a>, and remixed "<a href="/wiki/One_Love_(Nas_song)" title="One Love (Nas song)">One Love</a>" and "<a href="/wiki/It_Ain%27t_Hard_to_Tell" title="It Ain&#39;t Hard to Tell">It Ain't Hard to Tell</a>" for <a href="/wiki/Nas" title="Nas">Nas</a>' 10th anniversary edition of highly praised <i><a href="/wiki/Illmatic" title="Illmatic">Illmatic</a></i> album. He contributed to <a href="/wiki/Soundtrack" title="Soundtrack">soundtracks</a> to 2001 films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Fast_and_the_Furious_(2001_film)" title="The Fast and the Furious (2001 film)">The Fast and the Furious</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Osmosis_Jones" title="Osmosis Jones">Osmosis Jones</a></i>, and 2005 film <i><a href="/wiki/The_Longest_Yard_(2005_film)" title="The Longest Yard (2005 film)">The Longest Yard</a></i>.
</p><p>In 2014, Loftin, together with Fadale Northan and Frisco Rivera, produced the theme music and instrumentals for the former world champion boxer <a href="/wiki/Willy_Wise" title="Willy Wise">Willy Wise</a> workout DVD, <i>W3P: Willy Wise Workout - 3 Degrees of Power</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
