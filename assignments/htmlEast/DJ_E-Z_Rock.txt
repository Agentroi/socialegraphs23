<p><b>Rob Base &amp; DJ E-Z Rock</b> were an American <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> duo from <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a>, New York City. Rob Base is the stage name of Robert Ginyard (b. 1967) and DJ E-Z Rock was Rodney "Skip" Bryce (1967–2014). They are best known for the 1988 hit "<a href="/wiki/It_Takes_Two_(Rob_Base_%26_DJ_E-Z_Rock_song)" title="It Takes Two (Rob Base &amp; DJ E-Z Rock song)">It Takes Two</a>", a single that was a <a href="/wiki/Top_40" title="Top 40">top 40</a> hit and has been <a href="/wiki/RIAA_certification" title="RIAA certification">certified platinum</a> by the <a href="/wiki/RIAA" class="mw-redirect" title="RIAA">RIAA</a>. That song was a part of the duo's album of the same name, which also has been certified platinum. They are known for being pioneers of the crossover success that rap music would have in the popular music mainstream.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Base_%26_DJ_E-Z_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The duo's first U.S. single and release was "DJ Interview", appearing on World to World, which later got them a recording contract with <a href="/wiki/Profile_Records" title="Profile Records">Profile Records</a> in 1987. The duo was assisted by a long time friend from New Jersey, producer David Wynn. David Wynn produced three songs on their debut album and five songs on their sophomore album.
</p><p>The first Profile release was "<a href="/wiki/It_Takes_Two_(Rob_Base_%26_DJ_E-Z_Rock_song)" title="It Takes Two (Rob Base &amp; DJ E-Z Rock song)">It Takes Two</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It used multiple samples from the <a href="/wiki/James_Brown" title="James Brown">James Brown</a> and <a href="/wiki/Lyn_Collins" title="Lyn Collins">Lyn Collins</a> 1972 song "<a href="/wiki/Think_(About_It)" title="Think (About It)">Think (About It)</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The track first became a regional hit and then slowly climbed the <a href="/wiki/Billboard_Hot_100" title="Billboard Hot 100"><i>Billboard</i> Hot 100</a>, picking up a multi-platinum single certification. The song also peaked at No. 3 on the <a href="/wiki/Hot_Dance_Music/Club_Play" class="mw-redirect" title="Hot Dance Music/Club Play">Hot Dance Music/Club Play</a> chart.
</p><p>An album, also called <i><a href="/wiki/It_Takes_Two_(album)" title="It Takes Two (album)">It Takes Two</a></i>, was quickly put together and it produced a significant follow-up hit, "Joy and Pain", which sampled a song of the same name by <a href="/wiki/Maze_(band)" title="Maze (band)">Maze</a> featuring <a href="/wiki/Frankie_Beverly" title="Frankie Beverly">Frankie Beverly</a>, as well as "Put the Music Where Your Mouth Is" by <a href="/wiki/The_Olympic_Runners" class="mw-redirect" title="The Olympic Runners">The Olympic Runners</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It also hit the top 10 on the dance chart and climbed to No. 58 on the Hot 100. "Get On the Dance Floor", which sampled "Shake Your Body (Down To The Ground)" by <a href="/wiki/The_Jacksons" class="mw-redirect" title="The Jacksons">The Jacksons</a>, produced by David Wynn (DJ Wynn), a track released to clubs in between the two singles, hit No. 1 on the Hot Dance Music/Club Play chart in 1989. Boosted by those singles, the <i>It Takes Two</i> album went platinum seven times over.
</p><p>Base responded in 1989 with <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Base" title="The Incredible Base">The Incredible Base</a></i>, his debut solo album.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> It did not sell as well as <i>It Takes Two</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-LarkinDM_2-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LarkinDM-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> One song from the album hit the dance chart in late 1989: "Turn It Out (Go Base)", credited only to Rob Base.
</p><p>In 2008, their song "It Takes Two" was ranked number 37 on <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a>'s 100 greatest songs of hip hop.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_lives">Personal lives</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rob_Base_%26_DJ_E-Z_Rock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Personal lives"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Rob Base was born Robert Ginyard on May 18, 1967.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the <a href="/wiki/Fourth_grade" title="Fourth grade">fourth grade</a>, he moved from <a href="/wiki/The_Bronx" title="The Bronx">the Bronx</a> to <a href="/wiki/Harlem" title="Harlem">Harlem</a> where he became a classmate of DJ E-Z Rock.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He attended Harlem public schools and loved music. Influenced by rap, he began his journey and performed in talent shows and at as many open mic or hip-hop events as he could. His first child, De'Jené Ginyard, was born in 1989 to his then-girlfriend Rhonda Dunbar, with whom he was in a relationship from 1986 to 1990. In 1991, Base met April, and in 1992, they had a son, Robert Ginyard Jr. They subsequently took guardianship of April's cousin Dysell. Base and April married and remained together until her death in September 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>DJ E-Z Rock died on April 27, 2014, at age 46 after a diabetic seizure.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
