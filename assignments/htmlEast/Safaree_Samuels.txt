<p><b>Safaree Lloyd Samuels</b> (born July 4, 1981)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a Jamaican-American rapper. He started his musical career in the group the Hoodstars in the early 2000s alongside rapper <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>. Samuels is also known as a solo artist. In 2016, he joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> reality show <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_Hollywood" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood">Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood</a></i>. In 2017, he moved back to his hometown of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> and joined the cast of <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_New_York" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York">Love &amp; Hip Hop: New York</a></i>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life_and_education">Early life and education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Safaree_Samuels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life and education"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Safaree Lloyd Samuels was born in the <a href="/wiki/Brooklyn" title="Brooklyn">Brooklyn</a> borough of <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a> on July 4, 1981, to immigrant <a href="/wiki/Afro-Jamaicans" title="Afro-Jamaicans">Jamaican</a> parents. He has two sisters, Shaneequa and Samantha.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/wiki/Midwood_High_School" title="Midwood High School">Midwood High School</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Safaree_Samuels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Samuels started his musical career in the group the Hoodstars, formed in the early 2000s with rappers <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a>, Loustar, and Seven Up.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2004, the group recorded the entrance song for <a href="/wiki/Women_in_WWE" title="Women in WWE">WWE Diva</a> <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Marie_Varon" title="Lisa Marie Varon">Victoria</a>, "Don't Mess With", which was featured on the compilation album <i><a href="/wiki/ThemeAddict:_WWE_The_Music,_Vol._6" title="ThemeAddict: WWE The Music, Vol. 6">ThemeAddict: WWE The Music, Vol. 6</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> After the group disbanded, Minaj started her solo career and Samuels supported her as a <a href="/wiki/Hype_man" title="Hype man">hype man</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, he appeared in Minaj's music video "<a href="/wiki/Stupid_Hoe" title="Stupid Hoe">Stupid Hoe</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2012, Minaj released her second album, <i><a href="/wiki/Pink_Friday:_Roman_Reloaded" title="Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded">Pink Friday: Roman Reloaded</a></i>, where Samuels has six writing credits and was featured on the track "Press Conference".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Samuels released the mixtapes, <i>It Is What It Is</i> and <i>It Is What It Is, Vol. 2</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape1_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape1-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Mixtape2_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mixtape2-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> In early 2016, he made appearance in the second season of <i><a href="/wiki/K._Michelle:_My_Life" title="K. Michelle: My Life">K. Michelle: My Life</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In May 2016, it was announced that Samuels joined the cast of the <a href="/wiki/VH1" title="VH1">VH1</a> reality show <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_Hollywood" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood">Love &amp; Hip Hop: Hollywood</a></i> for its third season, which premiered in August 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In July 2016, he released a new mixtape titled <i>Real Yard Vibes</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-RYV_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RYV-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In August 2017, it was announced that Samuels would compete in the first season of VH1's <i><a href="/wiki/Scared_Famous_(TV_series)" title="Scared Famous (TV series)">Scared Famous</a></i>, which premiered in October 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2018, Samuels was honored with a Blue &amp; Bougie Impact Award.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In the same month, a nude picture and video of Samuels was leaked online.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2019, Samuels debuted the "Anaconda" line of <a href="/wiki/Dildo" title="Dildo">dildos</a> molded from his own penis.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> In 2020, Samuels launched an <a href="/wiki/OnlyFans" title="OnlyFans">OnlyFans</a> account sharing his <a href="/wiki/Amateur_pornography" title="Amateur pornography">amateur pornographic</a> videos for $20 per month.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Safaree_Samuels&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Samuels dated rapper <a href="/wiki/Nicki_Minaj" title="Nicki Minaj">Nicki Minaj</a> from 2000<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> until their breakup in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Several tracks on Minaj's third album <i><a href="/wiki/The_Pinkprint" title="The Pinkprint">The Pinkprint</a></i> are believed to have been inspired by the end of their relationship.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2016, he attempted to sue Minaj and accused her of not crediting him on <i>The Pinkprint</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He became engaged to actress <a href="/wiki/Erica_Mena" title="Erica Mena">Erica Mena</a> on <a href="/wiki/Christmas_Eve" title="Christmas Eve">Christmas Eve</a> 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> The couple married on October 7, 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On February 3, 2020, Samuels and Mena welcomed their daughter.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> On May 4, 2021, he announced that he and Mena were expecting their second child together.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On May 25, 2021, <a href="/wiki/TMZ" title="TMZ">TMZ</a> reported that Mena officially filed for divorce from Samuels.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On June 28, 2021, he announced the arrival of their second child, a boy.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> He's currently dating <i><a href="/wiki/Love_%26_Hip_Hop:_Miami" title="Love &amp; Hip Hop: Miami">Love &amp; Hip Hop: Miami</a></i> co-star <a href="/wiki/Amara_La_Negra" title="Amara La Negra">Amara La Negra</a>.
</p>
