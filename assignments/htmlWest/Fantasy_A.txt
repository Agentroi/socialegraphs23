<p><b>Alexander Leroy Hubbard</b> (born May 23, 1993), better known by his stage name <b>Fantasy A</b>, is an American rapper from <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington working in the <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">hip hop</a> genre, as well as a songwriter, actor, and author. He is best known for his extensive guerrilla marketing and performance in the film <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_A_Gets_a_Mattress" title="Fantasy A Gets a Mattress">Fantasy A Gets a Mattress</a>.
</p>
<meta property="mw:PageProp/toc" />
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fantasy_A&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Fantasy A was born in <a href="/wiki/Seattle" title="Seattle">Seattle</a>, Washington, where he was diagnosed with autism at the age of three.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> He attended <a href="/w/index.php?title=Thurgood_Marshall_Elementary&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Thurgood Marshall Elementary (page does not exist)">Thurgood Marshall Elementary</a> as well as <a href="/wiki/Cleveland_High_School_(Seattle)" title="Cleveland High School (Seattle)">Cleveland High School (Seattle)</a> before graduating from <a href="/wiki/The_Center_School_(Seattle,_Washington)" class="mw-redirect" title="The Center School (Seattle, Washington)">The Center School (Seattle, Washington)</a> in 2011. While in high school he gained an interest in literature and wrote three books later published as <i>School Rich</i> (2012), <i>Life in the Eyes of an Autistic Person</i> (2012), and <i>Life in the World of Gabe Fabens and Sage the Scholar</i> (2013).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Fantasy_A&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the mid-2010s Fantasy A began to gain notice in the Seattle area for his guerrilla promotional campaigns where he would cover every neighborhood in posters for his books and music. The campaigns were so extensive that it was covered by both <a href="/wiki/The_Stranger_(newspaper)" title="The Stranger (newspaper)">The Stranger (newspaper)</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_Weekly" title="Seattle Weekly">Seattle Weekly</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Seattle_Weekly_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle_Weekly-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-The_Stranger_(newspaper)_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-The_Stranger_(newspaper)-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-South_Seattle_Emerald_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-South_Seattle_Emerald-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2017 Fantasy A starred in the short film Fantasy A Gets Jacked, which marked the start of his work in film.<sup id="cite_ref-KNKX-FM_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KNKX-FM-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2019 he became a reoccurring guest on KJR-FM's The Wake Up Show where he reviewed movies.
</p><p>In 2023 Fantasy A starred in the feature film <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_A_Gets_a_Mattress" title="Fantasy A Gets a Mattress">Fantasy A Gets a Mattress</a>, based loosely on his life and sections of his memoir.<sup id="cite_ref-Kitsap_Smokestack_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kitsap_Smokestack-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-South_Sound_Magazine_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-South_Sound_Magazine-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The film gained a cult following, winning Best Narrative Feature at the Seattle Black Film Festival before going on to a sell out a twenty-screening-long run at The Beacon Cinema. The film continued to sell out screenings at festivals and indie cinemas<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-In_Their_Own_League_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-In_Their_Own_League-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-KUOW-FM_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KUOW-FM-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>—three times at <a href="/wiki/Grand_Illusion_Cinema" title="Grand Illusion Cinema">Grand Illusion Cinema</a>, three times at Central Cinema, at the <a href="/wiki/Seattle_International_Film_Festival" title="Seattle International Film Festival">Seattle International Film Festival</a> Film Center during <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Folklife_Festival" class="mw-redirect" title="Northwest Folklife Festival">Northwest Folklife Festival</a>, at the opening night screening at Northwest Film Forum's Local Sightings Film Festival, and at the opening night screenings at the Tacoma Film Festival.<sup id="cite_ref-KUOW_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-KUOW-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Seattle_Times_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Seattle_Times-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-My_Ballard_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-My_Ballard-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Runta_Somali_News_Magazine_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Runta_Somali_News_Magazine-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-High_Country_News_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-High_Country_News-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The film also opened the Silent City Film Festival in <a href="/wiki/Ithaca,_New_York" title="Ithaca, New York">Ithaca, New York</a> and won Best Narrative Feature at Local Sightings Film Festival, as well as Best PNW Narrative Feature at the Tacoma Film Festival.
</p>
